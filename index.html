Malitha Gunawardhana

I am a PhD student at University of Auckland, New Zealand and working on developing a novel AI-powered analysis to aid fibrosis-targeted therapy for atrial fibrillation. At the same time I work (contract) at IPPT-PAN, Poland as a researcher supervised by Dr. Norbert Zolek working on a supporting system for diagnosis of breast cancer lesions using ultrasonography and machine learning

Before that, I was a research assistant at MBZAUI, UAE as a research assistant supervised by Dr. Muhammad Haris Khan, of MBZUAI and Dr. Daniel Harari of Weizmann Institute of Science, Israel. My research was on self supervised learning in videos and network calibration.

I completed my Bachelor of Science (Hons) Degree from the Department of Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering, University of Moratuwa, Sri Lanka in 2021 specializing in Biomedical Engineering

My research interests are in computer vision, medical imaging and self-supervised learning.
